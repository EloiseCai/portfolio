Interaction/Visual Design

Nokia ION Group

Based in Ottawa, the Nokia ION Group delivers telecommunication applications for monitoring, managing and optimizing complex network resources.

I was part of the UX Design team as an Interaction/Visual design intern. The main project I worked on was designing a dashboard that displays all the data from the network infrastructure into a visually-organized layout, providing a convenient place for engineers to check the status of the network easily, hence building trust among them.

In addition, I also worked on small projects including researching and creating new interfaces that adapt to a new design system.

Role: Ideation, Design from mock-ups to pixel-perfect documentation

Client: Nokia

Date: 2019 Jan - Apr

Skills: Prototype, Visual Design, Interaction Design, Information Archtecture, Presentation

Define

During our initial ideation meeting, we had a conversation with the developers to understand the basic scope of the project, as to who, what and why are we designing this feature.

In addition, we printed out the list of key performance indicators (KPIs) and tried to understand the complex technical terminologies by asking the developers. Based on this information, we are able to reorganized the KPIs into a more understandable structure considering their characteristics and functional requirements.

Design Process

Based on the initial insights, I conducted some researches and created mock-ups to explore different ways to communicate and visualize each KPIs, by analyzing its features (relationship, comparison, distribution, composition).

After reviewing with the product managers, I started arranging these modules together into layouts, considering the grouping of information. This went through several iterations as I continued to improve and refine the design.

Process info graphic

Design in detail

To assure a clear hierarchy, we used titles and icons to separate two sections organized based on the need of the users, and used appropriate interaction style for each section.

Design in detail

In order to convey a coherent branding awareness across all applications, I also learned to think of design from the corporate's perspective. The new design had to fall into the existing branding guideline and be scalable for future usage. These are very important factors when designing for a larger company.

Pixel perfect documentation

In order to provide a guideline for the developers to build the component, I then created a detail documentation to specify interactions, hierarchy, responsiveness, etc.

Reflection

My experience at Nokia had taught me so many stuff I couldn’t learn at school. I had the chance to design part of the a product that would be used by thousands of users to help them make decisions efficiently. During this process, I learned to curate every design decisions to better serve the user’s needs, while adheres to an organized branding guideline. It’s also very exciting to see how my design resides in this larger system of business and software development cycle, which connects the dots between the real world and the design incubator.

I am very grateful for all the knowledge I have gained from my supervisor as well as all the friendly and talented designers in the UX team! I will carry on my journey with the knowledge to become a better UX designer.
