NetEase is one of the largest Internet and video game companies in the world. NetEase focuses on creating high-quality, visually appealing mobile games. It also partners with famous gaming companies such as Blizzard Entertainment to operate the Chinese version of their games.

In 2018, NetEase cooperated with Warner Bros. to design a new mobile game called Harry Potter: Magic Awakened. I am part of the design team as a UX designer for almost two years. Process1. Translate Game Designer Documents

Generally, I got new requirements from game designers on Mondays. I would carefully review their design documents and discuss every details and question I had. This process is vital as I can understand the motivation behind the functions, what the game designers are trying to achieve from their perspective and what kinds of players they are targeting. After having a basic brief of the What, Who and Why, I often start creating task flows or a content map if it's a complex function.

Process2. Moodboards and prototypes

When designing for game interfaces, different from general interface design, we are telling a story. So it's important for us to think of the background story to set a mood for our design first. In the case of this game, we are telling a story about a student who is studying in the Hogwartz School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. To get that part right, I need to gather materials from books, movies, arts to create a mood board of the atmosphere I am trying to create. This also helps when communicating with UI artists, game designers so that we are on the same page.

Sample Mood bard

Depends on the complexity of the functions, I used different approaches. For those functions that are building from scratch or have complex interactions, I often create low-fidelity wireframes first to try out different layouts, different ways of showing content. For those that are mainly visual-based, I would go ahead and create mid-fidelity prototypes in Photoshop.

Featured works

① Magic cart

The magic cart is one of the major places for players to buy props and packages. Because Harry Potter’s franchise game needs to follow a strict restriction, everything needs to make sense in this magic world. We first think of putting this shopping place at the Weasley‘s Wizard Wheezes. But it’s then refused by Warners’ Bros., for Weasley‘s Wizard Wheezes doesn’t sell out-of-world props like cards. We then proposed to invent a small mysterious shopping cart that is run by a talking puppet. Nobody knows where it came from. This avoids using new characters and existing shops but it also makes sense in the magic world.

During the implementation process, we gradually build the belief of this shop by adding interaction details, visual appearance, visual effects, and provide players’ motivation to buy and raffle by adding anticipations, suspense, and positive feedback.

② Student Club

Student club is a place for players to bond with their own houses, make friends, share thoughts about the game. It's originally designed to rely merely on the social system. Because the key point of this system is to create a clear interaction but less focus on the visual side, I choose to use task flow.
