Process

First, the course managers sent us a draft script (Step 1) and announced the start of the project! Based on the script, I first analyzed every sentence and tried to understand their meanings (Step 2).

After the script is analyzed, I would draw some storyboards, collect some video references, create demo icons in order to communicate my ideas to my supervisor
(Step 3). The whole project is then separated into smaller sections, my supervisor and I follow the same process to iterate the design every day (Step 4, 5).

Reflection

Although this job is not as design related as it sounds. But I think overall it's an interesting experience that helps me develop my skills. I learned to think from the branding perspective more as these courses are viewed by over 10,000 employees across China. It's vital for these videos, courses to represent and show this international, precise company image of PwC.

Another thing I learned is how to keep learners' attention in an interesting way. Because economics concepts can be dull, my supervisor always reminds me to think in a way that attracts the leaners and keep their attention using easy-to-understand metaphors and show-not-tell.

Not to mention my ability to work alone, to communicate also improved through this experience.
