TEDxSFU

TEDxSFU is a non-profit program that hosts local, self-organized events each year that bring people together to share a TED-like experience.

I was part of the 2017 team serving as the web developer. My job includes designing and creating the website and the program guide as well as updating contents on a bi-weekly basis independently.

For the year 2017, the theme selected was “Shift”. I based my design on the theme, aiming to provide a shifting new experience for the users.

Concept

In the light of TED's spirit of spreading profound ideas within the context of Vancouver, SFU and more importantly, the theme of 2017, SHIFT, I aimed at shifting users' experience and perspective when designing the website.

In order to do so, I wanted to explicitly show the idea of shifting visually and through interaction to build a connection between the website and the viewers. I then decided to use the following form of horizontal scrolling as well as the shifting text on the homepage (as above).

Style Decisions and Wireframe

During the early ideation phrase, I worked closely with the graphic designer and cooperate with each other to develop a basic style guide both for the website and the printing materials, in order to provide a consistent TEDxSFU brand awareness. In terms of color, We used the color of TED, black, mixed with the color of SFU, red, a delightful combination.

As for the font, we used the classic Helvetica and Inconsola to convey a classic feeling for the content, but add more subtle animation to highlight the theme of shift.

Then I created a few prototypes using Adobe illustrator to communicate the overall feeling I was trying to achieve for the website and the program guide. (**The program guide was created after the website but went through the same iteration)

I then distributed the design proposal to the team members on weekly meeting and gather feedback.

Coding from scratch

After the layout is confirmed within the team, I built the website from scratch using HTML, CSS and JQuery/JS. I also researched and made use of many libraries from Github like fullpage.js. After analyzing the logic of the code, I also customized the code by myself.

After the completion of the website, I am also responsible for maintaining, updating contents and managing files from the Godaddy Cpanel and Filezilla.

Major Challenge and Iteraion

When we started to sell tickets back in August, after some dicussions with the marketing team regarding ticket sale. I realized despite the design perspective, I need to think from the marketing perspective and closely work with them to boost ticket sale.

In order to align with the marketing plan on the social media, which is presenting a few promotion videos asking questions like "how do you define 'shift'? " So I decided to change the design of the homepage by including the video as the background to keep people's attention, while keeping the "shifting" interaction when people scroll down. This allows the audience to really see and absorb the content which attracted them to buy a ticket.

Reflection

Because at the beginning of this position, I wasn't experienced enough to code a bug-free website. The production circle of the first version of the webpage span around one month. However, the horizontal scrolling was not working very well on the mobile site. I then spent another month to recreate the code specifically for the mobile site.

If I were to do this website again, I would separate the code for mobile and web at the beginning to accommodate the specific design decision I made.
