A proposal of architectural form design within the context of Rotterdam

Blaak Station Rethink

This is a project of imagining us in the context of Rotterdam and redefine the form of the Blaak Station bearing the meaning of post-industrialism and creative economy in mind in a team of 5 people.

Through the project, I practiced my design and communication skills through multiple iterations of 3D forms that fit in the context and through lots of presentations that clearly communicate our ideas and decisions.

I think the project demonstrate and build up my skills to become a better designer and make better design decisions.

Role: Project Manager Art Director

Client: Spatial Design Course

Date: 2017 Sep- Dec

Skills: Form Design, Research, Presentation and Communication.

Research

During the first week, we first researched about the city, Rotterdam with consideration of its history and identity, its switch from industrialism to post-industrialism and some prominent architectural buildings that highlight the central idea of connectivity, from which we can learn from.

Dirty prototype and ideation

We then start ideating our own form through sketching based on three approaches(negative volumes, form analogies, clusters of "pods") from the architectural language. Then we picked some promising ideas and created lots of fast and dirty prototypes. Then we picked the most promising three models to further ideate.

Through these fast prototypes, I am able to physically vision the ideas that were in my head which allows me to quickly identified problems physically and the features that are most promising.

Sketches process

Dirty prototypes

We repeated this process several times by going to Seattle for 3 days to visit the famous architectures and created more fast prototypes and a consolidated form as well as present our ideas on the site.

The Seattle Charrette was intense, compared to previous ideation when we had a week to ideate, we only had three days which really forced me to prototype and make decisions really quickly.

My team's hard work and dedication paid off - we won first place in the Seattle Charrette.

Communication

When the form design was nearly concluded, we switch our focus onto organizing the communication of the final presentation including the visual representation, the flow, and the copywriting, while refining our form at the same time.

Designing the flow of communication

At this stage, we also proposed that the function of our form would be a train station that replace the formal Blaak station which fails to acknowledge the post-industrialism happening in Rotterdam.

I was responsible for researching about other stations and adapting it into our form, controlling the flow and the digital visual representation of the idea using the form of infographic.

Final pretation slide

Reflection and leadership

At the early stage of the project, we only need to practice one skill at a time. For example, we researched during the first week and designed form during the following week.

However, during the last part of the project, we need to use all the skills at once. We need to prototype, write copywriting, design form and design presentation at the same time, which requires all teammates to actively producing to the team.

I, as the project manager, acknowledged everyone's skill set and assigned tasks to make sure we create high-quality products and finish the project within the time frame.

More importantly, I learned from this project that I need to create prototypes and visualize my ideas in real life rather than designing in my head.
